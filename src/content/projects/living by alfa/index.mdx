## Context

**LINK Arkitektur** was invited into a dialogue-based design competition with **Alfa Development** for a new housing development in Søborg, on the outskirts of Copenhagen. The brief: find the balance between quality private homes and inspiring shared facilities, in a rational and efficient plan layout.

The proposal follows LINK's user-focused design method - engaging future residents in the creative process from the earliest phases, alongside specialists in interior design, computational design, and sustainability, so the functional and emotional needs of the people who'll actually live there shape the plan from the start.

## Methods

<figure>
  <LoadingImage src={concept} width={1600} alt="Diagram showing three standardized apartment types plus a stair and elevator core, combined into building clusters of two, three, or four apartments, then assembled into the full angular massing of Living by Alfa Søborg." />

Three standardized apartment types - a single/one-bedroom unit, a two-bedroom/two-bathroom shared unit, and a larger couple or family unit - combine with a shared stair and elevator core into clusters of two, three, or four homes. Mixing and matching those clusters lets the same modular system adapt to different target groups and site conditions, while standardization keeps the building economical to construct and operate.

<figure>
  <LoadingImage src={social} width={1400} alt="Diagram of three building plots in yellow, green, and blue, each labeled with its primary usergroup, connected by a path to a shared clubhouse for all residents of Living by Alfa Søborg." />
  <figcaption>Social organization by Martin Skou Nautrup: three building plots by usergroup, linked to a shared clubhouse.</figcaption>
</figure>

That mix carries through to how the site itself is organized: one plot for singles and shared apartments, one for mixed groups in larger shared homes, and one for couples and young families, all connected to a shared clubhouse that serves as a meeting place for every resident.

## Outcomes

Apartment type 1 - the single/one-bedroom unit - packs a built-in alcove bed, a combined kitchen and living space, and a compact bathroom into a small footprint, tested here in two layout variations.
