## Context

North of the river in Gothenburg lies Backaplan, a shopping-center district with existing plans to become a dense urban neighborhood. The stream Kvillebäcken runs through the area, and its northern reaches - largely anonymous businesses, paved surfaces, and heavy traffic - became this project's site: an edge to reconnect with the new Backaplan taking shape beside it.

Car travel dominates Kvillebäcken today at the expense of walking and cycling, producing noise, pollution, and long, exposed stretches between destinations. But the site has real assets to build from: strong transit connections now reinforced by the new Hisingsbron bridge, flat and open terrain well suited to cycling, and a stream that, properly supported, could carry far more ecological value than it does now.

## Methods

The concept - creating proximity while preserving space - reinterprets the superblock: a large block that routes car traffic around its perimeter while people on foot or bike move freely within and across it. Framed this way, the blocks become urban oases, their park-scaled interiors serving as both private and public recreation space. Small structures - greenhouses, local bike workshops, storage - give the resulting residential parks further spatial definition, while natural elements shape habitats for other species alongside the people using them. A new tram line and bus ring line extend this logic outward, reinforcing Swedenborgsplatsen as the area's transit hub.

Given the site's scale, the proposal builds in five phases, starting in the south where existing new development already ends and growing north from there: a school with a generous schoolyard first, then a phase built around urban gardening, then two phases that preserve much of the existing building stock - making room for a preschool, private courtyards, restaurants, and a mix of villas within the larger apartment blocks - before a final phase gathers new buildings around Kvillebäcken itself, where richer wildlife is expected to take hold alongside cafés, footpaths, and courtyards.

## Outcomes

At the scale of a single block, the superblock logic resolves into a perimeter of housing wrapped around a shared courtyard, its looping path connecting a play area to the surrounding green space.

In section, the block's housing steps from six storeys down to a lower wing framing the courtyard, with a playground pavilion and mature trees carrying the block's green space through to ground level.
