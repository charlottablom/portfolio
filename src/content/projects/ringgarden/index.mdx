## Context

Ringgården's second department sits at the crossing of Viborgvej and Ringgaden in Aarhus: a U-shaped open block built between 1939 and 1942, three storeys under a saddle roof, in red brick with white-detailed windows. Its 54 apartments, spread across 8 stairwells, have never had their own bathroom, and the kitchens are small. The street side faces a busy crossing; the courtyard side faces south, quiet and largely unused.

The brief was to bring the building up to a contemporary standard without losing what makes it recognizable - continuing, rather than replacing, a transformation already under way next door in the department's first phase.

## Methods

The street facade stays as it is - the building's recognizable face at an important crossing. Everything new happens on the courtyard side: a structural framework added along the inner facade, echoing the language used in the neighboring department's own transformation, extended upward into dormers to create better rooftop apartments. Balconies are woven into that framework throughout, bringing outdoor space, planting, and a livelier facade to what was previously a quiet, unused elevation.

Every apartment is reorganized to be through-lit, with its own kitchen-alrum, bathroom, and balcony; one stairwell gains an elevator, converting several units into accessible apartments. Layouts were tested in variations - the mid-stairwell's two-room unit, for instance, kept the same footprint of kitchen-alrum, entrance, bathroom, and bedroom across three variations, differing mainly in how the kitchen-alrum itself is arranged, with the living room always facing the quiet courtyard as the apartment's primary room.

## Outcomes

At ground level, the south-facing courtyard becomes what the brief called a small oasis: green and planted, with shared seating - value for each individual home, and for the department's community as a whole.
