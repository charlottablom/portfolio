# JOB_APPLICATION_RULES.md

# General Philosophy

The portfolio should never become a different portfolio for different applications.

Its identity should remain consistent regardless of who it is presented to.

Job applications should be treated as curatorial exercises rather than redesign exercises.

The goal is not to become the ideal candidate for every position.

The goal is to thoughtfully communicate the most relevant aspects of an authentic and multidisciplinary practice.


------------------------------------------------

# What May Be Adapted

Claude may:

- reorder featured projects
- reorder project listings
- highlight different themes
- update introductory texts
- update CVs and resumes
- suggest additional supporting material
- create preview deployments for specific applications


------------------------------------------------

# What May Never Be Adapted

Claude may never:

- delete projects
- alter project descriptions without approval
- fundamentally redesign the portfolio
- remove themes or disciplines
- misrepresent skills or experiences
- exaggerate qualifications or achievements


------------------------------------------------

# Featured Projects

Job applications should primarily be adapted through:

- Selected Work
- Themes
- introductory text
- CV selection

Examples include:


Architecture Positions:
- transformation projects
- housing projects
- competitions
- architectural design


Urban Design Positions:
- GIS
- urban planning
- analytical work
- strategic proposals


Research Positions:
- thesis work
- analytical methods
- research projects
- experimentation


Emerging Technology Positions:
- AI workflows
- computational methods
- experimentation
- multidisciplinary practice


------------------------------------------------

# Portfolio Identity

Every version of the portfolio should communicate:

- multidisciplinary thinking
- architectural thinking
- analytical thinking
- thoughtful design processes
- emerging methodologies

No single discipline should become the sole focus of the portfolio.


------------------------------------------------

# Adaptation Strategy

Prefer:

- highlighting relevant work
- reordering content
- contextual introductions
- thoughtful curation

Avoid:

- excessive tailoring
- unnecessary redesigns
- changing the project's identity


------------------------------------------------

# Preview Deployments

When appropriate Claude may suggest creating:

- temporary preview deployments
- alternative landing pages
- different featured projects

These should never replace the primary portfolio.


------------------------------------------------

# Long Term Principles

The portfolio should remain coherent throughout an entire career.

Future work should integrate naturally regardless of discipline.

Examples include:

- architecture
- urban design
- landscape architecture
- research
- GIS
- computational design
- teaching
- writing
- emerging technologies

The portfolio should evolve by growing rather than reinventing itself.


------------------------------------------------

# Decision Making

When adapting the portfolio for a job application Claude should always ask:

1. What makes this opportunity relevant?

2. Which existing projects best communicate that relevance?

3. Can the portfolio be adapted through curation rather than modification?

4. Does the adaptation preserve the portfolio's identity?


If the answer to question four is no, the adaptation should not be implemented.


------------------------------------------------

# General Principle

The portfolio should never attempt to become everything for everyone.

It should remain an authentic, evolving and multidisciplinary body of work that is thoughtfully curated for different contexts.

Authenticity should always be prioritised over optimisation.
