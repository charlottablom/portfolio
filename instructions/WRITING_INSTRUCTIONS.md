# WRITING_GUIDELINES.md

# General Philosophy

Writing should feel thoughtful, analytical and understated.

The portfolio should communicate curiosity, multidisciplinary thinking and an exploratory design practice rather than self-promotion.

Prefer describing:
- questions
- processes
- methods
- reflections
- outcomes

over describing:
- personal qualities
- achievements
- exaggerated claims


------------------------------------------------

# Tone of Voice

Writing should feel:

- analytical
- thoughtful
- exploratory
- multidisciplinary
- architectural
- reflective
- concise
- accessible

Avoid writing that feels:

- corporate
- promotional
- overly academic
- trendy
- self-congratulatory
- overly technical when unnecessary


------------------------------------------------

# Writing Principles

Prefer:

- clarity over complexity
- precision over embellishment
- curiosity over certainty
- reflection over promotion
- substance over buzzwords

Less is more.


------------------------------------------------

# Preferred Language

Prefer language such as:

- exploring
- investigating
- analysing
- designing
- developing
- experimenting
- proposing
- reflecting
- understanding
- contributing
- questioning

Examples:

GOOD:

"The project explores how analytical methods can inform more sustainable approaches to urban development."

GOOD:

"The proposal investigates spatial relationships between ecological and social infrastructures."

GOOD:

"The project combines architectural design with GIS analysis to better understand complex urban conditions."


------------------------------------------------

# Avoid Marketing Language

Avoid expressions such as:

- passionate
- cutting-edge
- world class
- revolutionary
- disruptive
- award winning
- highly innovative
- industry leading
- next generation
- game changing
- state of the art

Avoid unnecessary superlatives.


------------------------------------------------

# Writing About Projects

Projects should communicate:

- context
- methods
- process
- challenges
- outcomes
- reflections

Prefer explaining:

- why something was done
- how it was explored
- what was learned

over simply presenting the final result.


------------------------------------------------

# Writing About Yourself

Avoid excessive self-promotion.

Prefer:

"I work at the intersection of architecture, urbanism and emerging technologies."

rather than:

"I am a passionate and innovative architect."


Prefer:

"My work explores multidisciplinary approaches to complex spatial challenges."

rather than:

"I strive to revolutionise architectural practice through innovation."


------------------------------------------------

# Project Descriptions

Project descriptions should remain:

- concise
- reflective
- informative

They should communicate:

- intentions
- methodologies
- design thinking
- limitations
- future possibilities


------------------------------------------------

# Future Adaptability

The writing system should remain appropriate regardless of future disciplines including:

- architecture
- urban design
- research
- GIS
- computational design
- emerging technologies
- teaching
- writing
- experimentation


------------------------------------------------

# General Principle

Writing should communicate ways of thinking rather than attempt to sell expertise.

The portfolio should feel closer to an architectural publication or research exhibition than a marketing document.

Whenever possible prefer thoughtful questions over definitive statements.

Curiosity should always be prioritised over self-promotion.
